# Au Escapes the Ribbon Under Foundation MLIPs

**Status: Open**

## Claim

Gold (Au) is a special case: when foundation MLIPs are added, Au's error vector
*escapes* the hyper-ribbon (its participation ratio jumps), unlike the other 14 IMMI
elements which stay compressed.

## Evidence so far

- **MACE-MP-0:** Au escapes (PR 1.02 → 1.67); Pt compresses (1.29 → 1.03).
- **CHGNet:** Au escape **confirmed** independently.
- **Ag** was hypothesized to escape similarly — **refuted**: CHGNet pulls Ag back onto
  the ribbon. So the escape is Au-specific, not a noble-metal class effect.
- Reconciliation note: PR-based escape detection and within-style rank-correlation
  auto-eval are *orthogonal* lenses; both stand. Pearson r at n = 3 with wide property
  spread is ill-posed for escape detection — do not use it as the arbiter.

## Why it stays open

The effect is real and reproduced across two MLIPs, but the *mechanism* is unknown and
the noble-vs-refractory split (group means 0.90 vs 0.22) is a competing framing not yet
resolved. Confidence sits ~0.65 after the auto-eval reconciliation.

## Next

Add Orb-v3 / further MLIPs to test Au-specificity vs. a broader pattern; pin down a
mechanistic hypothesis (relativistic d-electron behavior is the leading candidate) and
state it falsifiably.
