# Fe Is a Persistent Outlier

**Status: Open — narrowed after Born-screened re-audit**

## Current status after Born screening

The old “Fe stays PR > 2 under every foundation-MLIP addition” line is not
currently citable. Born stability screening excludes **CHGNet-Fe** (C11 < |C12|),
so the original trio count used an invalid elastic tensor. Independently, at
n = 8–11 Born-stable models per element, Fe's cross-model alignment is **+0.80**
and the screened error axis remains one-dimensional rather than Fe-specific.

What survives is narrower: Fe remains a high-priority magnetic failure-mode
suspect because one foundation model's Fe tensor fails mechanical stability and
Fe appears as a MACE-disagreement sentinel in later cross-MLIP analysis. That is
a mechanistic target, not the original PR-outlier proof.

## Claim

Iron (Fe) is a high-priority magnetic failure-mode suspect for foundation MLIPs.
The old claim that Fe is the one IMMI element that remains off-ribbon across the
foundation-model trio is frozen until Born-stable recomputation.

## Historical evidence (pre-screening; do not cite as current PR evidence)

Across the foundation-MLIP trio (MACE-MP-0, CHGNet, Orb-v3), Fe was the held-out
1 of 15 in every pre-screening "14/15 on the ribbon" result. Its participation
ratio appeared to stay **PR > 2** regardless of LAM addition. This paragraph is
preserved for provenance, but the current claim must be recomputed after Born
screening.

## Why it stays open

The original invariance claim is not well-established after screening; the
*failure mode* remains compelling. Fe's magnetism (BCC ferromagnetic ground
state, complex spin-dependent bonding) is the obvious suspect, but that is a
hypothesis, not a result. It is also entangled with the [BCC > FCC residual](bccfcc-causal-shield.md)
and the [Au escape](au-mlip-escape.md) — the three "special element" findings may
share a mechanism.
