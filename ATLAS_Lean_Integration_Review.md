## 1. Executive Summary

Meta's ATLAS-Lean (Autoformalized Textbook Library At Scale) has been open-sourced, providing a substantial Lean 4 library of textbook mathematics autoformalized by LLMs. This document presents the integration architecture for incorporating ATLAS-Lean into Lupine's Science Lean Distiller (`lean-spec`), significantly expanding our formal proof capabilities across analysis, algebra, geometry, topology, and probability theory.

The integration enables Lupine to move beyond our current 47 proven theorems toward a comprehensive mathematical foundation that underpins materials science formalization. By importing ATLAS modules as Lake dependencies, we gain access to 25+ subject domains of pre-formalized mathematics while maintaining compatibility with Mathlib conventions.

### Key Benefits

| # | Benefit | Primary ATLAS Module |
|---|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | Accelerated formalization of PDE-based LAMMPS trace requirements | `IntroductionToPartialDifferentialEquations` |
| 2 | Strengthened manifold geometry proofs | `DifferentialGeometry`, `GeometryOfManifolds` |
| 3 | Rigorous statistical foundations | `HighDimensionalStatistics`, `TheoryOfProbability` |
| 4 | Pathway to AutoformBot pipeline | `facebookresearch/autoform-bot` |
| 5 | Formal foundations for multi-agent hypothesis generation | `RealAnalysis`, `AlgebraNotes`, `TensorCategories` |
| 6 | Observability integration for autoformalization traces | `OpenInference` semantic conventions |

### Integration Surface

This document covers six integration layers:

- **lean-spec**: Direct Lake dependency import of ATLAS theorems into our formal specification
- **glim-think**: ATLAS formal foundations feeding multi-agent durable object facets (ManifoldFacet, CausalFacet, TheoristFacet, ExperimentFacet)
- **Phoenix OTel + OpenInference**: Tracing and evaluating LLM-driven autoformalization with OpenInference semantic conventions
- **atlas-distill**: Rust distillation engine consuming formalized mathematics
- **distiller (ODF)**: Open Distillation Factory orchestration harness
- **library-site**: Publication and visualization of formalized theorem inventory

The library draws from undergraduate and graduate textbooks spanning multiple mathematical domains, providing reusable formal building blocks for human- and machine-driven formalization.

## 4. Integration Architecture

### 4.1 Lake Dependency Model

ATLAS-Lean can be integrated into Lupine's lean-spec through Lake's `require` mechanism. The recommended approach:

```toml
[[require]]
name = "Atlas"
git = "https://github.com/facebookresearch/atlas-lean"
rev = "<pinned-rev>"
```

This approach provides:

- **Clean namespace separation**: `Atlas.*` for imported mathematics, `OpenDistillationFactory.*` for our materials science formalization
- **Reproducible builds** through pinned revisions
- **Selective import**: only import the ATLAS modules we need, keeping build times manageable
- **Upgrade path**: bump the rev as ATLAS improves coverage and Mathlib alignment

## 5. Specific Upgrade Paths

### 5.1 Analysis.Manifold Enhancement

**Current state**: 10 theorems proving participation ratio bounds and hyper-ribbon claims. Relies on ad-hoc manifold definitions for differentiable potential energy surfaces.

**ATLAS upgrade path**:
- Import `Atlas.DifferentialGeometry` and `Atlas.GeometryOfManifolds` for formalized manifold definitions, tangent bundles, and smooth map compositions
- Replace custom definitions with ATLAS's formalized `RealAnalysis.Differentiable` and `Topology.Basic` imports
- Target theorem expansion: from 10 to ~25 theorems covering Riemannian metric structures on configuration spaces
- Leverage `Atlas.HighDimensionalStatistics` for concentration inequalities applied to participation ratio bounds

### 5.2 Theory.ParameterBound Strengthening

**Current state**: 1 theorem proving PR <= min(params, observables) for differentiable potentials.

**ATLAS upgrade path**:
- Import `Atlas.RealAnalysis` for formalized supremum/infimum properties, continuity, and compactness arguments
- Import `Atlas.IntroductionToFunctionalAnalysis` for operator norm bounds on potential energy functionals
- Use `Atlas.TheoryOfProbability` for probabilistic bounds when parameters are treated as random variables
- Target: Generalize from differentiable potentials to Lipschitz-continuous and Sobolev-class potentials

### 5.3 Computation.LammpsTrace PDE Foundation

**Current state**: 3 theorems covering trace requirements for benchmark entries. Limited mathematical foundation for MD simulation correctness.

**ATLAS upgrade path**:
- Import `Atlas.IntroductionToPartialDifferentialEquations` for formalized conservation laws (energy, momentum) that govern MD simulations
- Import `Atlas.FourierAnalysis` for spectral analysis of velocity autocorrelation functions
- Build formal connection between PDE well-posedness and LAMMPS numerical stability
- Target: 8-10 theorems linking continuous PDE solutions to discrete MD timestepping correctness

### 5.4 UniversalityBridge Algebraic Foundation

**Current state**: Recently added UniversalityBridge module connecting universality theorems to context-specific proofs. Needs stronger algebraic foundations.

**ATLAS upgrade path**:
- Import `Atlas.AlgebraNotes` and `Atlas.AlgebraicCombinatorics` for formalized group/ring theory applied to symmetry-based potential invariants
- Import `Atlas.TensorCategories` for formal tensor manipulation matching MLIP tensor contraction patterns
- Use `Atlas.LieGroups` for formalizing symmetry groups of crystal structures and their representation in potential energy models
- Target: Complete the algebraic foundation for the Conditional Universality Theorem with formally verified symmetry constraints

## 7. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| Mathlib revision mismatch | MEDIUM | Pin to ATLAS revision; maintain compatibility branch |
| Build time increase | LOW | Cache ATLAS artifacts in CI; selective imports only |
| `sorry` proofs in ATLAS Unproved | LOW | Only import `Atlas` (proved) target; avoid `Unproved` |
| Namespace collision | LOW | Strict namespace discipline: `Atlas.*` vs `OpenDistillationFactory.*` |
| API breakage on ATLAS updates | MEDIUM | Pin rev; update in dedicated PR with full rebuild |
| Team learning curve | LOW | Phase 1 provides time for team to study ATLAS patterns |
| glim-think DO state growth | MEDIUM | Store only theorem references, not full proofs, in Durable Object state |
| Phoenix trace volume | LOW | Sampling for autoformalization traces; full capture for evals only |
| TorchSim GPU availability | LOW | Graceful CPU fallback; batched CPU still faster than ASE serial |

**Overall risk level: LOW-MEDIUM**. The Lake dependency model provides clean isolation, and the phased approach allows rollback at each stage.

## 8. ATLAS-Lean + glim-think Multi-Agent Integration

### 8.1 Architecture Overview

glim-think is Lupine's Project Think integration — a serverless, multi-agent infrastructure running on Cloudflare that moves the `atlas-distill` Rust engine from an ephemeral CLI into durable, persistent infrastructure. The system is organized around four specialized Durable Object facets:

```
                    OrchestratorThink
          (Model routing, agenda, dispatch)
           /                    \
    RPC  /                        \  RPC
       /                            \
ManifoldFacet                   CausalFacet
(SQLite + R2)                   (SQLite + R2)
     |                                |
     | RPC                            | RPC
     v                                v
TheoristFacet                  ExperimentFacet
(Session fork/tree)            (Tier-4 Sandbox:
                                LAMMPS + git)
```

**The ATLAS-Lean integration feeds formalized mathematical foundations into each facet's durable state**, transforming glim-think from a heuristic-driven research assistant into a formally-grounded scientific reasoning system.

### 8.2 Facet-by-Facet Integration

#### ManifoldFacet + ATLAS DifferentialGeometry

The ManifoldFacet manages differentiable geometry reasoning for potential energy surface exploration. Currently, it relies on ad-hoc mathematical heuristics. With ATLAS-Lean:

- **Import `Atlas.DifferentialGeometry`** formal definitions of manifolds, charts, atlases, and smooth maps into the facet's SQLite state
- **Store theorem references** (not full proofs — keep DO state bounded) for key results: smoothness of coordinate transitions, tangent space properties, pullback operations
- **Enable formal verification** of manifold exploration results: when the facet identifies a critical point or saddle, reference ATLAS theorems on Morse theory and index calculations
- **Target**: ManifoldFacet RPC responses include `formal_basis: Atlas.DifferentialGeometry.*` citations alongside numerical results

#### CausalFacet + ATLAS TheoryOfProbability

The CausalFacet handles statistical causality detection and Simpson's paradox identification (already a strength of Lupine's `Analysis.Causal` with 9 theorems). ATLAS extends this:

- **Import `Atlas.TheoryOfProbability`** for formalized probability spaces, random variables, expectation, and convergence theorems
- **Import `Atlas.HighDimensionalStatistics`** for concentration inequalities (Chernoff, Hoeffding, Bernstein bounds) applied to causal inference confidence
- **Strengthen Simpson's paradox detection** with formal bounds on confounding variable impact
- **Target**: CausalFacet can formally bound the probability of false causal attribution given observational data size

#### TheoristFacet + ATLAS AlgebraNotes / TensorCategories

The TheoristFacet manages hypothesis generation and session tree exploration with a fork/tree model (BYOM — Bring Your Own Model). ATLAS provides the algebraic foundation:

- **Import `Atlas.AlgebraNotes`** for formalized group/ring/field theory applied to symmetry-based potential invariants
- **Import `Atlas.TensorCategories`** for formal tensor manipulation matching MLIP tensor contraction patterns (ACE, MTP, SNAP descriptors)
- **Import `Atlas.LieGroups`** for formalizing crystal point groups and space groups as Lie group representations
- **Enable formal hypothesis validation**: new hypotheses are checked against algebraic constraints before entering the experiment pipeline
- **Target**: TheoristFacet session trees include `algebraic_validity` gates powered by ATLAS theorems

#### ExperimentFacet + ATLAS IntroductionToPDEs

The ExperimentFacet runs LAMMPS simulations in a Tier-4 sandbox with git-tracked workspaces. ATLAS closes the gap between continuous PDE theory and discrete MD practice:

- **Import `Atlas.IntroductionToPartialDifferentialEquations`** for formalized conservation laws: energy conservation, momentum conservation, Nose-Hoover thermostat correctness
- **Import `Atlas.FourierAnalysis`** for spectral analysis of velocity autocorrelation functions, phonon density of states
- **Build formal PDE-to-MD bridge theorems**: link well-posedness of continuous governing equations to stability of Verlet/LEAPFROG integrators
- **Target**: ExperimentFacet simulation results include `pde_wellposedness` certification from ATLAS theorems

### 8.3 OrchestratorThink Integration

The OrchestratorThink routes tasks between facets using AI Gateway with multi-provider model selection. ATLAS integration at the orchestrator level:

- **Theorem availability registry**: Orchestrator maintains a registry of which ATLAS theorems are loaded in which facet's SQLite state
- **Formal capability routing**: Route tasks requiring formal verification to facets with the relevant ATLAS modules loaded
- **ATLAS version tracking**: Orchestrator tracks ATLAS revision pin across all facets for consistency
- **Model selection update**:

| Task | Preferred Model | Fallback | ATLAS Context |
|------|----------------|----------|---------------|
| Formal proof synthesis | `@cf/moonshotai/kimi-k2.5` | `gpt-4.1` | ATLAS theorem statement |
| Manifold exploration | `@cf/meta/llama-3.3-70b` | `claude-3-7-sonnet` | DifferentialGeometry refs |
| PDE verification | `@cf/meta/llama-3.3-70b` | `gpt-4.1` | IntroToPDEs conservation laws |
| Statistical causality | `@cf/meta/llama-3.1-8b` | `@cf/mistral/mistral-7b` | Probability space defs |

### 8.4 State Management Strategy

Critical constraint: Durable Objects have limited state size. ATLAS integration uses a **reference-don't-store** pattern:

```
DO SQLite State (per facet):
  atlas_revision      TEXT  -- pinned ATLAS git rev
  mathlib_revision    TEXT  -- matching Mathlib rev
  theorem_inventory   JSON  -- {theorem_name: {module, statement_hash, status}}

Full ATLAS library:
  Stored in R2 bucket, mounted as read-only Lake dependency

Agent runtime:
  Lake imports available in sandbox build environment
  Theorem references resolved at build time, not DO state
```

### 9.4 Phoenix Eval Integration

glim-think's `evals/` system already runs hypothesis-lifecycle tracing and scientific-throughput evaluations. ATLAS integration adds formal verification quality evals:

#### Eval 1: Autoformalization Success Rate

```python
# evals/atlas_autoformalization_success.py
from phoenix.evals import LLMEvaluator

def autoformalization_success_rate(traces):
    """
    Measures the ratio of autoformalized theorems that pass
    lake build without sorry proofs.
    """
    autoform_spans = [t for t in traces
                      if t.attributes.get("lupine.autoformalization.source")]
    successes = [t for t in autoform_spans
                 if t.attributes.get("lupine.proof.status") == "proved"]
    return len(successes) / len(autoform_spans) if autoform_spans else 0
```

**Target**: >70% autoformalization success rate (no `sorry`) for ATLAS-adjacent theorems

#### Eval 2: Mathlib Compatibility Score

```python
# evals/atlas_mathlib_compatibility.py
def mathlib_compatibility_score(traces):
    """
    Measures whether autoformalized theorems build against
    both ATLAS's pinned Mathlib and Lupine's master Mathlib.
    """
    compatibility_spans = [t for t in traces
                           if t.attributes.get("lupine.build.target")]
    dual_builds = [t for t in compatibility_spans
                   if t.attributes.get("lupine.build.dual_mathlib_ok")]
    return len(dual_builds) / len(compatibility_spans) if compatibility_spans else 0
```

**Target**: 100% dual-build compatibility for all imported ATLAS theorems

#### Eval 3: Theorem Dependency Depth

```python
# evals/atlas_dependency_depth.py
def avg_theorem_dependency_depth(traces):
    """
    Tracks average dependency tree depth for proofs using
    ATLAS theorems. Deeper = more sophisticated formalization.
    """
    verify_spans = [t for t in traces if t.kind == "EVALUATOR"]
    depths = [t.attributes.get("lupine.theorem.depth", 0) for t in verify_spans]
    return sum(depths) / len(depths) if depths else 0
```

**Target**: Average dependency depth increases from 1.2 (current) to 3.0+ (with ATLAS)

## 10. The Closed Scientific Loop

### 10.1 The Full Loop Architecture

Lupine is not a collection of independent tools. It is a **closed scientific reasoning system** where simulation results drive hypothesis generation, hypotheses drive formal proof, proofs drive test validation, and validated tests feed back into better simulations. ATLAS-Lean is the mathematical foundation that closes this loop.

## 11. TorchSim MLIP Benchmarking Pipeline

### 11.1 Problem Statement

The current Lupine MLIP test pipeline is brittle. It relies on a combination of legacy LAMMPS invocations, ad-hoc Python scripts in `lupine-distill/tests/`, and manual benchmark coordination. Key problems:

- **No pre-distillation baseline**: We cannot answer "what was this model's raw accuracy before we distilled it?"
- **No per-version uplift tracking**: Each distillation pass (v1, v2, v3...) produces a new model, but we lack systematic per-model, per-version benchmark deltas
- **No batched GPU execution**: Tests run serially through ASE, wasting GPU capacity and CI time
- **No unified benchmark format**: Results scatter across JSON files, CSVs, and CI logs with no schema

### 11.2 TorchSim as the Replacement

**TorchSim** (`torch-sim-atomistic`) is a PyTorch-native atomistic simulation engine purpose-built for the MLIP era:

- **100x speedup** over ASE through batched GPU simulation
- **Native MLIP support**: MACE, FairChem, SevenNet, ORB, MatterSim, metatomic, Nequix
- **Automatic batching**: `autobatcher=True` fills GPU memory with concurrent systems
- **Pure Python**: Integrates directly with PyTorch autograd for differentiable benchmarks
- **Trajectory reporting**: Built-in H5 trajectory format with arbitrary property tracking
- **CI-friendly**: pip-installable, no compilation step, no LAMMPS binary dependency

### 11.3 Benchmark Architecture

The new pipeline uses a **benchmark-before-distill** pattern with per-model version tracking:

```
                    MLIP Model Registry
                    (per-model metadata)
                           |
        +------------------+------------------+
        |                                     |
   TorchSim                                 Distiller
   Benchmark                                (ODF/atlas-distill)
        |                                     |
        v                                     v
   baseline_results/                    distill_v_results/
   {model_id}_v0.json                   {model_id}_v{N}.json
        |                                     |
        +------------------+------------------+
                           |
                    Uplift Calculator
                    (distill_v_uplift)
                           |
                           v
                  OperatorPack Promotion Gate
                  (passes if uplift > threshold)
```

### 11.4 Benchmark Suite Specification

Each MLIP model runs a standardized benchmark suite before and after each distillation pass:

| Benchmark | Description | Metrics | Systems |
|-----------|-------------|---------|---------|
| `static_energy` | Single-point energy on relaxed structures | MAE vs DFT (eV/atom) | FCC Cu, BCC Fe, diamond Si |
| `geometry_opt` | Cell + position relaxation to equilibrium | Force MAE, stress MAE, lattice const error | 5 elemental crystals |
| `nvt_md_300k` | NVT Langevin MD at 300K for 1ps | Energy drift, temperature stability | 256-atom Cu supercell |
| `nvt_md_1000k` | NVT Langevin MD at 1000K for 1ps | Same as above, high-temp stability | Same |
| `elastic_constants` | Voigt notation elastic tensor | C11, C12, C44 errors vs exp | FCC Cu, BCC Fe |
| `phonon_dos` | Fourier transform of velocity autocorrelation | Peak position error, linewidth | 256-atom supercell |
| `eos_curve` | Energy-volume curve sampling | Bulk modulus error, equilibrium volume | Isotropic compression |
| `surface_energy` | (111), (100), (110) surface energies | Surface energy MAE vs DFT | FCC Cu slabs |

### 11.5 The `distill_v_uplift` Metric

The core metric for measuring distillation effectiveness:

```python
def distill_v_uplift(model_id: str, baseline_v0: dict, distill_vN: dict) -> dict:
    """
    Computes per-model, per-distillation-version uplift across all benchmarks.
    Returns structured uplift report for OperatorPack promotion decision.
    """
    report = {
        "model_id": model_id,
        "distill_version": N,
        "baseline_version": 0,
        "overall_uplift": {},
        "per_benchmark": {},
        "promotion_recommendation": None,
    }

    for benchmark_name in BENCHMARK_SUITE:
        v0_metrics = baseline_v0[benchmark_name]
        vN_metrics = distill_vN[benchmark_name]

        # Composite: weighted average of normalized metric improvements
        improvements = {}
        for metric_name, weight in BENCHMARK_WEIGHTS[benchmark_name].items():
            v0_val = v0_metrics[metric_name]
            vN_val = vN_metrics[metric_name]
            # Lower is better for MAE/error metrics
            pct_improvement = (v0_val - vN_val) / abs(v0_val) * 100
            improvements[metric_name] = {
                "baseline": v0_val,
                "distilled": vN_val,
                "pct_improvement": pct_improvement,
                "weight": weight,
            }

        weighted_score = sum(
            imp["pct_improvement"] * imp["weight"]
            for imp in improvements.values()
        )

        report["per_benchmark"][benchmark_name] = {
            "weighted_uplift_pct": weighted_score,
            "metrics": improvements,
            "passes_gate": weighted_score > MIN_UPLIFT_THRESHOLD,
        }

    # Overall: average across benchmarks
    overall = sum(
        b["weighted_uplift_pct"]
        for b in report["per_benchmark"].values()
    ) / len(report["per_benchmark"])

    report["overall_uplift"] = {
        "pct": overall,
        "passes_gate": overall > MIN_UPLIFT_THRESHOLD,
    }
    report["promotion_recommendation"] = (
        "promote" if overall > MIN_UPLIFT_THRESHOLD else "reject"
    )

    return report
```

**Gate thresholds**:

| Gate | Threshold | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| `promote` | overall_uplift > +5% | Model promoted to OperatorPack artifact |
| `review` | overall_uplift between 0% and +5% | Manual review required |
| `reject` | overall_uplift < 0% (regression) | Distillation failed, investigate |

```python
            span.set_attribute("lupine.gcp.runner", "mlip-cell-runner")

            span.set_attribute("lupine.benchmark.system", system)
            span.set_attribute("lupine.benchmark.suite", benchmark)
            span.set_attribute("lupine.results.mae_energy", results["mae_energy"])
            span.set_attribute("lupine.results.mae_forces", results["mae_forces"])
            span.set_attribute("lupine.results.wall_time_s", results["wall_time"])
            span.set_attribute("lupine.results.gpu_utilization", results["gpu_util"])

            # Link to formal predictions from lean-spec
            predictions = self.get_formal_predictions(backend, system)
            span.set_attribute("lupine.theorem_predictions", json.dumps(predictions))

            # Evaluation: did results match formal predictions?
            span.set_attribute("lupine.prediction.match",
                self.evaluate_predictions(predictions, results))
```

## 15. archive/KIMI_MLIP_UNIVERSAL Research Bundle

### 15.1 What It Is

The `archive/KIMI_MLIP_UNIVERSAL` directory in Lupine contains the **Conditional Universality Theorem for Error Geometry in Machine-Learning Interatomic Potentials** — a research bundle formalizing the universal properties that all well-behaved MLIPs must satisfy regardless of architecture. This is Lupine's most ambitious formalization effort.

### 15.2 ATLAS Strengthening

The universality theorem benefits directly from ATLAS integration across multiple modules:
